A Computing PhD student researching IoT/iot at the Imperial College of London.

Learn

I'm a keen software engineer/computer scientist, having just finished my MSci in Glasgow, now starting a PhD at Imperial College London, and very interested in all things related to operating systems, wireless sensor networks, embedded/contrained devices, robotics, vehicular ad-hoc networks, distributed systems and many others.

I also like learning languages, such as French and Japanese (also dabbled in Italian, German, Cantonese).

Projects

I love working on projects, especially ones involving electronics, code and physical bits and bobs. During my last 2 years of Uni, I've worked on two significant self-defined projects, which involved creating a secure and integrated Intranet of Things protocol and suite of tools to support it.

Work

I've been lucky enough to work for two great companies, Fujitsu and JP Morgan, whilst studying for my degree.

I worked at the Fujitsu's Human Centric Robotics Lab in Atsugi, Japan and got to experience an amazing country for three months. Whilst there I got to meet some great and very friendly people, enjoyed the wonderful weather (lovely sun, oh and a typhoon, earthquake and many sweaty commutes), worked with a fantastic team on an very cool teddy bear robotics project, check him out here. I also wrote a little blog about my trip here.
